# Outreach Message Templates

5 templates for reaching out to potential clients — without sounding like cold outreach.

The rule: lead with something specific about THEM, not your services.

---

## Template 1: The Specific Observation (LinkedIn / Email)

Use when: You've read something they wrote or built.

```
Hi [Name],

I read your post about [specific topic]. The part about [specific detail] was exactly what I needed to hear.

Quick question: are you [working on / planning] any projects right now where [your service area] plays a role?

Either way, great content. I'll be following along.

— [Your name]
```

---

## Template 2: The Referral Path (Email)

Use when: A mutual contact suggested you reach out.

```
Hi [Name],

[Referral name] mentioned you might be worth connecting with — she said you're working on [general project area].

I help [type of client] with [specific result you deliver]. If there's a fit, I'd love to hear what you're working on.

Happy to jump on a 20-minute call if you're curious.

— [Your name]
```

---

## Template 3: The Former Client Introduction (LinkedIn / Email)

Use when: You're reaching out to someone in the same space as a past client.

```
Hi [Name],

I recently finished a project with [type of company, not specific name] in [industry/niche]. We [specific outcome — e.g., rewrote their onboarding sequence and cut drop-off by 20%].

I'm looking to take on one or two similar projects in the next quarter and wanted to reach out before I fill my schedule.

Would it make sense to spend 20 minutes exploring whether there's a fit?

— [Your name]
```

---

## Template 4: The Content Compliment (Twitter/X DM or LinkedIn)

Use when: They posted something you genuinely found useful.

```
Hey [Name] — your thread on [topic] is the clearest thing I've read about this.

I'm a [your role] and this just changed how I think about [specific thing].

Not pitching anything — just wanted to say it was genuinely helpful.
```

*Note: This is not a pitch. It's relationship building. Follow up 2–3 weeks later if they respond.*

---

## Template 5: The Direct Ask (Email — for warm leads only)

Use when: You've had prior interaction or they've shown interest.

```
Hi [Name],

I've been following your work for a while and I think what you're building with [their project/company] is exactly the kind of thing I want to be working on.

I specialize in [specific skill] for [type of company]. Here's a quick example of my work: [1 link or 1 sentence description].

If you have 20 minutes in the next two weeks, I'd love to learn more about what you're working on and see if I can help.

Would [date/time] work?

— [Your name]
```

---

## Usage Notes

- Never send more than 2 outreach messages to the same person in 30 days
- Always personalize the first line. Generic openers get ignored.
- If no response after 2 messages, move on
- Track your sends in a simple spreadsheet: Date | Name | Template | Response (Y/N)
