# Proposal Template

A clean, one-page proposal structure that reduces "I'll think about it."

Rule: Short is better. If your proposal is longer than 2 pages, cut it.

---

## Proposal Structure

---

**[Your Name / Business Name]**
**Proposal for [Client Name]**
**Date: [Date]**
**Valid for: 14 days**

---

### What We're Solving

[1–2 sentences summarizing the client's core problem, in their words from the discovery call.]

Example:
*"You're growing your newsletter faster than expected, but your onboarding sequence isn't converting subscribers into buyers — and you're not sure why."*

---

### What I'll Deliver

**[Project Name]**

Deliverables:
- [Deliverable 1 — specific and concrete]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]

What's not included:
- [Anything out of scope — be explicit]
- [Additional rounds of revisions beyond X]

[Brief description]

**Phase 2 — [Name] (Week 3–4)**
[Brief description]

**Review + Delivery (Week 5)**
[Brief description]

## Notes on Using This Template

**On pricing:**
- Never send a proposal without a clear price
- If you're unsure, give a range: "Between $X and $Y depending on..."
- Don't apologize for your price

**On scope:**
- List what's NOT included as carefully as what IS
- One round of revisions is standard. Two rounds is generous. Three is where projects go sideways.

**On follow-up:**
- If no response in 5 days, send one follow-up
- If no response to follow-up, close the loop: "I'll assume this isn't the right fit for now. If that changes, feel free to reach out."
- Don't chase. It signals desperation and lowers your value.

**On the 14-day expiry:**
- Real. If a client can't decide in 14 days, they're not ready — or they're waiting for a cheaper option. Either way, you should know.
