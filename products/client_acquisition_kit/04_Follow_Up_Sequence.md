# Follow-Up Sequence

3 emails for prospects who've gone quiet after a proposal or conversation.

Rule: Maximum 3 follow-ups. After that, close the loop and move on.

---

## When to Use This

Use after:
- A proposal has been sent with no response in 5+ days
- A discovery call that ended with "I'll get back to you"
- A conversation that felt promising but stalled

Do not use after:
- A clear "no" (respect it, don't chase)
- A price objection (that's a different conversation)

---

## Email 1 — The Simple Check-In

**Send:** 5 days after proposal or last contact
**Subject:** Re: [Original subject line]

```
Hi [Name],

Just checking in on the proposal I sent over on [date].

Any questions, or is there anything I should clarify?

Happy to jump on a call if it's easier.

— [Your name]
```

*Short. No pressure. Just checking.*

---

## Email 2 — The Value Add

**Send:** 5 days after Email 1 (10 days after proposal)
**Subject:** One thing that might help with [their problem]

```
Hi [Name],

I know you're weighing the decision on [project], so I wanted to share something that might be useful either way.

[1 paragraph: share a relevant insight, resource, or example related to their specific situation — not a pitch, just something useful]

Still happy to move forward on [project] when the timing is right. Let me know if you have questions.

— [Your name]
```

*The goal is to be useful, not to push. This positions you as an expert, not a vendor.*

---

## Email 3 — The Close

**Send:** 7 days after Email 2 (17 days after proposal)
**Subject:** Closing the loop on [project name]

```
Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up one last time on the proposal for [project name].

If the timing isn't right or priorities have shifted, that's completely fine — I just want to make sure I'm not holding space on my calendar unnecessarily.

If you'd like to revisit this in the future, just reply and I'll pick up where we left off.

Wishing you the best with [their project/business].

— [Your name]
```

*This is your off-ramp. It sounds final because it is. Some clients respond to this one when they ignored the first two — the finality triggers action.*

---

## What to Do After Email 3

If no response: Close the opportunity in your tracker. Move on.

If they respond positively: Treat it as a new conversation. Don't guilt them for going quiet.

If they come back weeks later: This is common. Reply warmly, check if your rate/timeline still works, and proceed.
